Green tea, black tea, and white tea come from the same tea plant, Camellia sinensis. It's just that the leaves are processed differently. Green tea leaves are not fermented, while black and white tea undergo a crushing and fermentation process.

The big difference between black tea and green and white tea is that black tea is much more oxidized. This means that black tea has more color, flavor, and higher levels of caffeine. This oxidation process also means that black tea can retain its quality and flavor for years longer than other types of tea.

Green Tea

Green tea, with the Latin name Camellia sinensis, is a popular beverage in Indonesia, Japan, and China. Green tea is a type of tea that has undergone minimal oxidation during processing. The shorter processing time results in a milder flavor profile and helps maintain the integrity of beneficial chemical compounds.
